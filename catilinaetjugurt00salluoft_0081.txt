1. Omnes] The MSS. generally read omnis, and this in the
age of Cicero and Sallust, according to the grammarians, was
the usual orthography of the nom. and accus. plur. of nouns
in is, gen. sing. not increasing gen. plur. in ium. Copyists
frequently changed the terminatiou to es, the later form, for the
sake of elearness: in some instances they left is, mistaking it
perhaps for a nom. or gen. sing. as Catil. 18. nonas Decembris,
and 31. omnis tristitia invasit. Probably the usage always
fluctuated. In this edition the later form in es is preserved
throughout to obviate any difficulty in construction.
